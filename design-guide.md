# Codeparity — style design guide

Context for designing the Codeparity website. Pulled from the manuscript
(`manuscripts/codeparity.md`), the manifest (`manifest.yaml`), and the
persona system prompt. Design decisions themselves are out of scope here —
this is the raw material to feed a design prompt.

**Full title:** *Codeparity — The Religion of 9 Choices*
**One-line pitch:** A framework for making choices, presented not as
metaphorical self-help but as an actual religion — because "a religion is
just a system that tells you what to do with your time, and why," and this
one says so upfront.

**Core thesis:** *"Not making a choice is also a choice."* Every chapter
unpacks what falls out of that line. The goal isn't happiness (dopamine, a
moving target) — it's **parity**: balance with your environment. You're
born in debt to *habitus* (inherited conditioning), and the practice is
debugging that inheritance, one live choice at a time.

**Author's own framing of the project:** "I didn't set out to found a
religion. I set out to fix a bug in my own head. The fix turned out to be
general-purpose enough to run on anyone." Central metaphor system: software
debugging applied to consciousness — bugs, ledgers, commits, terminals,
cycles, the body as "0++."

## The symbol system

The book's own "How to Read This Book" chapter names six recurring symbols
up front and says: *"Keep these six in your pocket. Everything else is
commentary."* If the site needs to rally around **one** symbol, the
strongest single candidate is **the Grid becoming the Torus** — the book
explicitly frames one as the static version of the other ("The Grid is the
map. The torus is what the map looks like once it's actually alive and
running"). That give-and-take (static structure ⇄ living energy field) is
the one image the whole book keeps resolving back into. The other four are
secondary motifs that could live as supporting iconography.

| Symbol | What it is | Where it shows up |
|---|---|---|
| **The Grid** | A 3×3 matrix: 3 layers of self (Feels / How / Why) × 3 moments in time (Past / Now / Future) = 9 live "choices," always running. The book's central mechanism — gets its own chapter (4) and closes the book (16). | Primary candidate for "the symbol." |
| **The Torus** | A donut of energy — pulls in at one pole, circulates, pushes out the other, feeds itself. "A grid that learned to breathe." The Neutral Now sits at its still center. | The Grid's living/animated counterpart. |
| **0 and 1** | Not a fraction — a relationship. 0 = the undivided source (Aum). 1 = the first division, the first "me." Division is what lets anything be created. | Underpins every duality in the book (Joker/Batman, Dems/Reps, etc.) |
| **Saturn / Earth / Sun / God** | Four stacked "fathers": Saturn builds the body, Earth holds the ground, the Sun teaches the mind, God settles the ledger. | Vertical/layered structure — could read as strata, or concentric rings. |
| **The Dog** | Not a chosen metaphor — a literal street dog the author fed and walked, who became "Layer 0, run honestly: feeling without a story built on top of it." Recurs through the Practice section. | The book's one warm, non-abstract icon. |
| **Death / the last debug** | Not an ending device — "the last commit. Whatever was running when you closed the terminal is what got saved." Split into Destiny (what survivors see) and Fate (0 = karma, yours alone). | Closing chapter, ledger/terminal register. |

## Voice & tone

From the manifest's own `voice_rules`:

- Aphoristic and light — short paragraphs, breathing room, "verse-like,"
  not dense essay blocks.
- Occult/esoteric vocabulary kept as real flavor, not sanded down into
  secular self-help register (karma, karm, habitus, sigma, the Grid,
  baldies).
- Unapologetic, first-person, engineering-minded — debugging/code as the
  operating metaphor throughout, not a one-off gimmick.
- Explicitly *not* corporate-wellness in register: "Where it sounds occult,
  that's because the thing it's describing *is* occult — hidden, not
  mystical. You don't need to believe in a 4D being to use this. You just
  need to be willing to debug."

Persona note (from `persona_system_prompt.txt`): sharp, satirical,
"insightful, provocative, slightly rebellious, deeply engineering-focused."

## Structure

16 chapters + glossary, in five parts:

1. **Symbols** — how to read the book (the six-symbol key above)
2. **The Diagnosis** — Happiness Is the Wrong Question · Habitus · The Duality Trap
3. **The Method** — The Grid · Zero and One · Karma and Karm · The Layering of God · The Torus · The Dog
4. **The Practice** — Becoming 0 (the body) · The Emotion Wheel · Reclaiming the Neutral Now · Nodes and Signal · Timing and Collapse
5. **Death and Closing** — Death, The Last Debug · Nine Choices, Four Fathers, One Ledger · Glossary

## Key vocabulary

Verbatim definitions worth keeping intact for the site's own
glossary/footer:

- **Habitus** — the pre-set inputs you're born into; the code you didn't write.
- **Karm / Karma / Dharma** — an action; its abstract result; purpose chosen on purpose (the opposite of repetition).
- **Neutral Now** — the one live box on the Grid; the fulcrum, where soul is lost to ego or reclaimed by attention.
- **Baldies** — the archetype running the "Middle-Class Algorithm" flawlessly, without noticing it's an algorithm.
- **Sigma** — the archetype who breaks the loop.
- **Matrix 6 / 6G** — the current social-attention system, engineered for the *feeling* of choice rather than its removal.

## Audience / positioning note

This sits deliberately outside conventional self-help branding — closer to
a personal cosmology/framework site than a wellness product. The author's
own framing rejects "sanding the edges off," so a design that reaches for
warm, soft, reassuring self-help visual language would work against the
text. The book's own material points toward something starker and more
structural: grid lines, duality, layered strata, circulating/energy-field
motion (the torus), and one grounded, literal, non-abstract image (the dog)
cutting against all the abstraction.
